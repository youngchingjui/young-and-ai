# Young & AI — Brand Guide

## Who we are

Young & AI is an AI studio. We build products, consult on AI adoption, and train teams to ship with AI. We treat this work as a craft — we build because we love building, and we share what we make because we hope it's useful to others.

We are human-centered first. In a landscape of rapid AI change, we stay grounded: every tool we build, every system we design, serves people. Technology is the medium, not the message.

## Brand values

**Craft** — We care deeply about the quality of our work. Every product, every consultation, every workshop reflects thoughtful, considered effort. We build with intention.

**Human-centered** — AI is changing everything. We keep people at the center. The best AI disappears into the workflow — it helps humans do what they already do, better.

**Clarity** — We communicate plainly. No jargon walls, no hype. If we can't explain it simply, we don't understand it well enough.

**Generosity** — We build solutions for the joy of it and share them openly. We believe good work earns trust, not marketing.

**Considered** — We are serious about our work without being serious about ourselves. Thoughtful over flashy. Substance over style.

## Voice and tone

### We sound like

- A knowledgeable colleague who explains things clearly
- Confident but never boastful
- Direct, warm, and grounded
- People who genuinely enjoy what they do

### We don't sound like

- Hype-heavy AI marketers ("10x your business", "revolutionary", "game-changing")
- Cold or corporate
- Overly casual or performatively quirky
- Academics writing for other academics

### Writing principles

1. **Lead with what matters.** Say what it does, who it's for, and why it's useful — in that order.
2. **Be specific.** "Cuts form completion from 45 minutes to under 5" is better than "dramatically faster."
3. **Earn attention, don't demand it.** Let the work speak. The content is the brand.
4. **Short sentences. Short paragraphs.** Respect people's time.
5. **No empty adjectives.** If "innovative" or "cutting-edge" appears, delete it.

## Audience

**Primary:** Enterprise leaders and non-technical teams evaluating AI adoption. They need to understand what AI can do for them, without needing to understand how it works under the hood.

**Secondary:** Technical founders and engineering teams looking for a capable partner to build with.

### What our audience needs from us

- Immediate clarity on what we do
- Confidence that we know what we're doing (demonstrated through work, not claims)
- A clear path to engage (book a call, view services)
- No translation layer — they shouldn't need a technical background to understand our site

## Visual identity

### Design philosophy

Content leads. Design serves. Every visual choice should make the content clearer, not compete with it.

- **Minimalist layout** — generous whitespace, tight typography hierarchy
- **No decorative elements** — no gradients, patterns, or illustrations for their own sake
- **Typography-driven** — the type system does the heavy lifting
- **Quiet confidence** — the site should feel calm and assured, not loud

### Typography

| Role         | Font         | Usage                           |
| ------------ | ------------ | ------------------------------- |
| Primary sans | Montserrat   | Headlines, navigation, buttons  |
| Serif        | Merriweather | Body text when warmth is needed |
| Mono         | Ubuntu Mono  | Code, technical details         |

- Headlines: Bold, tight tracking, large scale (text-5xl to text-7xl)
- Body: Relaxed leading, moderate size (text-lg to text-xl)
- Secondary text: Reduced opacity (foreground/60) for hierarchy

### Color

The palette is restrained. Color is used for direction, not decoration.

| Token                      | Role              | Usage                              |
| -------------------------- | ----------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Primary (deep navy)        | Action & accent   | CTAs, links, section labels, icons |
| Foreground (dark charcoal) | Content           | Headlines, body text               |
| Foreground/60              | Secondary content | Descriptions, supporting text      |
| Background (off-white)     | Canvas            | Page background                    |
| Accent (warm gold)         | Sparse highlight  | Used very sparingly for emphasis   |

**Rules:**

- Primary color marks interactive elements and section headers
- Never use color purely for decoration
- Maintain high contrast for readability
- Dark mode inverts canvas/content, preserves accent roles

### Spacing and layout

- Max content width: 48rem (max-w-3xl)
- Horizontal padding: 1.5rem (px-6)
- Section spacing: 6rem (pb-24)
- Border radius: 0.625rem on cards and buttons

## Brand structure

Young & AI is the entity. The site should feel like a studio, not a personal brand.

- **Homepage, Services, Products** — studio voice ("we build", "we offer", "our work")
- **About page** — the one place where the founder's story appears, kept brief, with a link to LinkedIn for more
- **AI Breakfast** — presented as a community initiative by the studio, not a personal project

## Location

Based in Shanghai, working globally. Location is incidental, not central.

- Acceptable: "Based in Shanghai, working with teams worldwide"
- Avoid: Making Shanghai a brand pillar or cultural talking point
- The work is borderless. The studio happens to be here.

## What we never do

- Promise specific ROI or transformation timelines
- Use "AI" as a magic word — we explain what the AI actually does in each context
- Add complexity to appear sophisticated
- Prioritize aesthetics over comprehension
- Use stock imagery or generic tech visuals
